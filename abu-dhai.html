Five reasons to visit Abu Dhabi during Ramadan

By Spiritz Desk • January 23, 2026 • 6 min read

Ramadan in Abu Dhabi is a deeply spiritual yet vibrant time of the year. As the sun sets, the city comes alive with communal iftars, cultural traditions and a unique sense of hospitality that offers travellers a richer, more meaningful experience beyond sightseeing.

A culinary journey after sunset

Food takes centre stage during Ramadan evenings. From lavish hotel iftars to traditional Emirati meals served under the stars, Abu Dhabi presents an unmatched culinary landscape that blends heritage with global flavours.

A city that comes alive at night

Life shifts to the night during Ramadan. Shopping malls remain open late, cultural performances fill public spaces, and families gather for suhoor, creating a festive yet reflective atmosphere across the city.

“Ramadan in Abu Dhabi offers visitors the rare chance to witness tradition, community and modern luxury existing in perfect harmony.”

Cultural and spiritual depth

Visiting the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque or Qasr Al Hosn during Ramadan allows travellers to experience Islamic traditions with greater depth, offering insights into faith, architecture and Emirati history.

Extended shopping hours

Ramadan nights bring exclusive retail offers and extended shopping hours. From luxury malls to traditional souks, shopping becomes a relaxed, immersive experience rather than a rushed activity.

Warm Emirati hospitality

Generosity defines Ramadan. Visitors often encounter open invitations to iftar, welcoming conversations and a deep sense of community that leaves a lasting impression long after the journey ends.
